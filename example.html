Problem 1

A sample of 10 bolts has lengths (cm):

4.8, 5.0, 4.9, 5.1, 4.7, 5.0, 4.9, 5.2, 4.8, 5.1.

Assume normality. Construct a 95% CI for the mean length.

1 / 11

Solution - Problem 1

Data: $n = 10$, $\bar{x} = 4.95$, $s = 0.158$
Confidence Level: $1 - \alpha = 0.95 \implies \alpha/2 = 0.025$
Degrees of Freedom: $df = n - 1 = 9$
Critical Value (from Table V): $t_{\alpha/2, df} = t_{0.025, 9} = 2.262$

Formula (from Section 8.2, Eq. 8.16): $$ \bar{x} \pm t_{\alpha/2, n-1} \left( \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \right) $$ Calculation: $$ 4.95 \pm 2.262 \left( \frac{0.158}{\sqrt{10}} \right) $$ $$ 4.95 \pm 2.262 (0.050) $$ $$ 4.95 \pm 0.113 $$ 95% CI: $\mathbf{(4.837, 5.063)}$
